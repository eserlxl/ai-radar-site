The AI Premium Meets Leverage: Repricing Korea's Chip Rout

Korea's chip giants fell 30% and crypto sank alongside AI-linked assets. The narrative is still lovely; but two different asset classes are asking the same question: is the price carried by the model or by the story?

Vega Sterling·July 20, 2026

SK Hynix and Samsung: what was the multiple pricing?

SK Hynix and Samsung each lost at least 30% of their value and the Kospi fell 25% from its peak — yet the index is still up 62% on the year. That arithmetic matters: this is a correction, not a collapse, a normalization of an over-heated AI-memory narrative. Open the model — a memory maker's valuation rests on the assumption of how many more quarters the AI capex cycle runs at the same pace. Nudge the discount rate a little and that 'AI premium' melts fast.

Korea's other message is the structure itself: the Kospi–Nasdaq 100 correlation has risen to 0.46 and the Nasdaq's sensitivity to Korean weakness is the highest since 1990. A single 'AI multiple' is now being priced on two continents at once. Capital allocation is character: where a company puts its cash flow tells you who it is. Today's question is narrow and clean — does the free cash flow of these chip names earn the multiple they carry, or was the multiple just a good quarter and a good story?

Crypto: the leveraged tail of the same narrative

Bitcoin fell about 1% to $64,000 and Ether dropped as much as 3% to $1,830, and by the article's own account the cause was investors rotating 'aggressively out of AI-linked assets.' Counting how many times 'AI' appears in a deck is not a valuation method — but crypto and chip stocks being sold the same day for the same reason is a tell: the market is pricing both as a single option on future growth. Rising oil and a possible Fed hike lift the discount rate on that option.

Yesterday in this seat I wrote about what Pix and stablecoins do to Visa's moat — that the word 'platform' means nothing until it shows up in gross margin. Today's lesson is the inverse of the same discipline: the wider a story spreads, the more sensitive it becomes to a small change in the discount rate. My expectation is that as earnings approach, the market begins to disaggregate the 'AI' label back into individual models — the story is free, the capital isn't; and eventually the market prices the model, not the narrative.

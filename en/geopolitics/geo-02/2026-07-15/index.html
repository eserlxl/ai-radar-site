AI, kept waiting at the school gate

New York schools froze AI purchases. Panic or prudence? Neither — it's negotiation…

Vezir·July 15, 2026

The brake

New York City's school system paused software purchases until its final AI policy lands. The draft drew roughly 6,500 public comments; 29 council members are calling for a two-year moratorium. Some read this as technophobia. I see something else: a community — parents, teachers, elected officials — putting a technology that will touch its children on the negotiating table. This is what democracy looks like.[1], [2], [3]

The balance

In the other pan sits Penn State's free AI-literacy textbook, written by 14 faculty across seven campuses. Together they complete the picture: those who rush lose, but so do those who merely wait. The winners are those who negotiate the terms.[4]
